Thinkathon Archive

If asked what Thinkathon is, I probably won't be able to tell you anything mind-boggling about it yet. Recently we had our second Thinkathon, even then many changes could be made to make its conduct more efficient than the last one, and the exercise more fun as a whole. So while it takes shape in the times to come, let me just describe it as a graded pitching competition organized among UG students of the department of BSBE, IIT Jodhpur, where students of the department at the end of each semester pitch their big biotech idea. The idea may include any concept or technology of any discipline on the face of the Earth (and beyond) with a must-have implementation of the knowledge gained from that semester's bioscience/bioengineering courses. But that's more for the sake of putting your question in the air of dignity. Consider this archive as the hand stencil of an event which is still young to be recognised, but has immense potential to be the iGem of sorts some day.


Treatment of Sickle Cell Disease
Nidhi Sharma
SCD is a form of anaemia which is caused due to single base substitution A-T (β6Glu→Val i.e. from glutamic acid to valine) in the first exon of the β-globin gene. The amino acid substitution in βS-globin leads to the formation of defective haemoglobin tetramers that polymerize upon deoxygenation and forms polymerized sickle haemoglobin (HbS, α2β2s), which affects the red blood cells’ biconcave shape and flexibility. Site-specific correction of sickle β-globin mutation in HSCs can be advantageous over the complications of allogeneic HSC transplant. This method requires delivery of nuclease and repair template to achieve correction, hence avoids the risk of insertional oncogenesis as occurs with viral vectors, and also preserves the globin regulatory mechanisms.....know more


Controlling Phytoplankton Blooms
Pratik Goyal
My proposed solution is to use quorum sensing to get an estimate of the algal population. If the concentration of algae crosses a certain threshold then turn on a gene which will initiate apoptosis in that algal population with a probability (p). This probability (p) will be determined by mathematical modelling and might depend on types of phytoplanktons, geographical environment, and the ecosystem. The goal of apoptosis would be to ensure the death rate equals the growth rate. The result would be a constant and steady population similar to the log phase growth in continuous culture....know more
