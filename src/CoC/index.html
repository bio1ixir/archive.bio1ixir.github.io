Code of Conduct

We have pivoted. Bio1ixir is now the class website of the BSBE-IITJ UG class of '22. Find our code of conduct below.

Code of Ethics and Standards.

Our Goal

This website serves as a showcase by curating all the awesome content (including but not limited to projects, write-ups, publications and art-work) concieved by the students of undergraduate class of 2022 in the department of Bioscience and Bioengineering, IIT Jodhpur.

General Ethics

Be respectful

Despite of its small size, inevitably there will be people with whom you may disagree, or find it difficult to cooperate. Accept that, but even so, remain respectful. Disagreement is no excuse for poor behaviour or personal attacks, and a community in which people feel threatened is not a healthy community.

Assume good faith

Bio1ixir contributors have many ways of reaching our common goal of reducing intellectual distance of its followers and contemporary works in Biotechnology. And their way may differ from your ways. Assume that other people are working towards this goal.

Note that our followers may have different levels of knowledge in biology. Our goal is to cut down their intellectual gap. So consider this while making content that you inadvertently don’t add more to their sufferings. At the same time, make sure you don’t oversimplify anything that it completely loses its meaning.

Be collaborative

The project has just set sail. The code is clean and comprehensible even if you have basic knowledge of web development and designing. But if you still feel uneasy while studying the repositories and want to collaborate on github, I encourage you to ask for help when you need it. Likewise, offers for help should be seen in the context of our shared goal of improving Bio1ixir.

When you make something for the benefit of the project, be willing to explain to others how it works, so that they can build on your work to make it even better.

Guidelines for collaboration

Visit our Contributors Manual.

Try to be concise

Keep in mind that what you write once will be read by hundreds of persons (hopefully). Writing a short email means people can understand the conversation as efficiently as possible. When a long explanation is necessary, consider adding a summary.

Try to bring new arguments to a conversation so that each mail adds something unique to the thread, keeping in mind that the rest of the thread still contains the other messages with arguments that have already been made.

Try to stay on topic, especially in discussions that are already fairly large.

Be open

Most ways of communication used within the club allow for public and private communication. You should preferably use public methods of communication for club-related messages/announcements, unless posting something sensitive.

This applies to messages for help or Bio1ixir-related support, too; not only is a public support request much more likely to result in an answer to your question, it also makes sure that any inadvertent mistakes made by people answering your question will be more easily detected and corrected.

Be Authentic

We are a community in science. Sharing authentic information should be our utmost concern. Subtulism is not an option.

While stating some dynamic stats in your post, that are subjected to changes with time, you must clearly mention the date/year till which the stats held truth. Moderation team will take care that every post with dynamic stats should stand this condition.

In case of problems

While this code of conduct should be adhered to by participants, we recognize that sometimes people may have a bad day, or be unaware of some of the guidelines in this code of conduct. When that happens, you may reply to them and point out this code of conduct. Such messages may be in public or in private, whatever is most appropriate. However, regardless of whether the message is public or not, it should still adhere to the relevant parts of this code of conduct; in particular, it should not be abusive or disrespectful. Assume good faith; it is more likely that participants are unaware of their bad behaviour than that they intentionally try to degrade the quality of the discussion.

Anti plagiarism policy

We consider plagiarism an academic dishonesty and a breach of journalistic ethics. Any kind of content theft and plagiarism is highly condemnable. We encourage all contributors to respect others Intellectual Property. And for some reason if you feel necessity to copy someone’s work, you are advised to well quote it within quotes (alternatively make it under code section.) and give the courtesy to the author alongside.

If our moderation team finds out any signs of plagiarism, your post shall be updated with due courtesy and removal of your name from the Author tag. Not only this, you’ll be blacklisted from posting on Bio1ixir if felt necessary.

How to report

Any kind of report can directly be reported at bio1ixir.iitj@gmail.com - or alternatively you can report concerning bodies instead.

Report a Bug: If you find a bug in any service provided by Bio1ixir, report it to the concerning repository of our organisation on github. Please follow the Issue template (if provided) while reporting. It’ll help the developer to access it easily.

Report Plagiarism: If you as a reader find that the some author has violated our anti-plagiarism policy (please read that before reporting) then Functional Manager for affairs related to plagiarism can be reported.

Report Incorrect Information: If you spot some inauthentic information in some post, Functional Manager for content moderation can be reported.
